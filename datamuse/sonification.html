Data Sonification?

What is Data Sonification?

In data visualizations, we turn a database of entries into a picture, tying specific heights, colors, or shapes to attributes and columns of the data (big numbers become tall bars, etc.). In data sonification, we can tie those same columns to aspects of music, such as volume, tempo, pitch, and instrumentation.

Why use Data Sonification?

Data visualizations, infographics, and comprehensive charts are popular right now because they express complicated ideas in simple, engaging ways. However, our bodies collect and process information in so many ways, not just through eyes. Many species around the world rely on scent, hearing and touch to navigate spaces, but we live in a digital world built almost exclusively with vision.

How does it work?

We've used the musicpy package in Python, which can match text strings of beats and note names into sound files. As we grab column, we can rewrite them in the syntax of musicpy then mix the results all together into a single music arrangement.
